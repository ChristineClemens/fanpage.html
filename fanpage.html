Coffee: The Divine Nectar of the Gods

A Coffee Appreciation Blurb

Stocked away among cans of tuna and packages of crumbled Melbatoast, most cupboards conceal a common vice: coffee, a staple of nearly every modern household. Whether it be brewed via French press or traditional filter, for the energy spike or nourishment of our inner brooding artist, these seemingly magic beans are a favourite among most.

An Appetizingly Ambiguous History

Although many of coffee's various preparation methods and sources are well documented today, much of its history stems from a speculative origin. With certainty, it can be said that coffee's heritage can be traced back, over several centuries, to the abundant coffee bean plants occupying the Ethiopian plateau, the exact time frame being unknown.

It is believed that Kaldi, an Ethiopian goat herder, first discovered the coffee bean and the lively benefits of caffeine after observing his goats consume the berries from a certain tree, later to be coined coffea. His herd exhibited spikes in energy so excessive that it often prevented sleep.

These findings were reported to the abbot of the local monastery, who was later responsible for concocting a drink from the berries identified, allowing him to remain energetic and alert during the practice of evening prayer. This discovery was eventually shared with other members of the monastery, an inside secret that very quickly spread by word of mouth and was soon carried across the globe.
